🧭 The question I keep returning to

The life science, biotech, and AI worlds are full of genuinely remarkable technology. But there's a persistent gap: brilliant research often stalls on the way to real-world use. Across academia, a startup, a Fortune 500, and consulting, my work has circled one question —

How do we help powerful technology cross the gap from research to real-world adoption?

Everything below is a version of that question being asked in a different domain.

🌱 Where it started — genomics meets modern agriculture

My first answer came from the field. Using evolutionary genetics and the latest sequencing technology, I worked on advancing modern crop breeding — taking research-grade science and turning it into something a breeding program could actually run on.

  • Built a digital platform to process and structure genomics information at scale
  • Developed machine learning models that turn genomic data into breeding decisions
  • Shipped it to production on AWS, partnering directly with a breeding company to put the system to work in the real world

This was the template for everything since: take a hard scientific idea, build the infrastructure around it, and get it adopted by the people who need it.

🚀 What I'm exploring next

Two frontiers I'm genuinely excited about:

🤖 Connecting the “smart brain” to the physical world. Generative and agentic AI are becoming capable reasoning engines. The next step is wiring that intelligence into physical AI and robotics — especially humanoid robots — so it can act in the world, not just think about it.

🧬 A language model for the genome. The same principles that made large language models work for human language can be applied to DNA. I want to help build a large genome model — a DNA sequence language model — that learns the grammar of biology itself.

🛠️ How I approach adoption

My method is consistent regardless of the technology:

  1. Start from first principles. Understand how the technology actually works before deciding what to do with it.
  2. Get hands-on. Build real things; earn intuition that no slide deck can give.
  3. Create a mechanism for adoption — for myself, my team, and the broader community — so the understanding scales beyond any one person.

During implementation, I lean on a cross-disciplinary framework to keep ambitious projects structured and deliverable:

  • Consulting principles — framing, structure, and value
  • Modern software development best practices — building things that last
  • Architecture principles — designing systems that scale
  • Project management principles — turning intent into delivery
  • The human–AI relationship — keeping people at the center
  • Professional consistency — the same standards, every time
